DowntimeTruth is building agent-based uptime verification for a market full of stale status pages, shallow polling, and noisy crowd-sourced trackers. We behave like real users, verify real breakage, and give software teams a lightweight path from /health checks to full critical-flow monitoring.

Problem

The internet has an uptime truth gap.

Production software is becoming more brittle at the exact moment teams are shipping faster than ever. Status pages are often PR artifacts. Third-party outage sites lean on weak probes or community chatter. Operators end up trapped between under-reporting and misinformation.

  • Status pages usually update after support tickets pile up.
  • Crowd-sourced outage trackers are noisy, delayed, and easy to game.
  • Conventional uptime checks miss broken sign-in, checkout, search, and AI workflows.
Thesis

Reliability needs verification, not vibes.

Observe like a user

Agents click, submit, log in, and move through key flows instead of checking a single port.

Stay lightweight

Smaller teams can start with a single /health endpoint for basic server uptime.

Align incentives

Monitoring should become more serious when the failure becomes expensive.

Product

Three layers of truth.

  • Health checks: ping /health for basic availability.
  • Agent flows: crawl and test what real users actually do.
  • Failure receipts: preserve evidence about where the flow broke.
Why Now

AI-native shipping changes the failure curve.

Vibe-coded products, tiny engineering teams, and faster deployment loops mean more product surface area can break without anyone noticing immediately. Reliability is no longer a back-office concern. It is growth, trust, and retention.
